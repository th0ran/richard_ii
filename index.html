Guidance for submitting questions

During the Q&A, David Tennant and Gregory Doran will be answering questions submitted by schools across the country. Questions can be submitted between 8.30 – 9.30 and during the scheduled breaks. Questions should not be submitted during the broadcast itself.

Only one question of 140 character limit can submitted at a time. When you are ready to submit a question click on the question button on the left (show the button) To give your school’s questions the best chance of being selected, try to ensure that they are about the performances, the production, the directors choices, or the play itself. It may help to ask students to write their suggestions down on paper as they watch the production , before selecting the best ones to be submitted online.

Information For Your IT Team

To access the broadcast we recommend the latest version of your browser (Internet Explorer, Chrome etc.) and the latest version of Adobe Flash. Please contact your ISP System Administrator about possible firewall restrictions, filters or blocks on streaming media. The test stream will be an important opportunity for you to identify any technical issues in advance of the event.

Broadcast Recomendations

We recommend that the broadcast is viewed on one large screen per classroom of up to 30 students, with a maximum of 10 computers accessing the broadcast per school in total. Please bear in mind that additional screens will use up more of your school's bandwidth and may result in buffering. To access the stream, teaching staff should be logged in to their workstation or laptop, preferably as either a 'power user' or as one with local administrator privileges.

Test Event

There will be a test event on 8th November to allow schools to check their technical requirements.

More details will be sent out to schools next month.

Compatibility With Mobile Devices

Please note that the classroom stream is not intended to be viewed on mobile devices such as iPads, iPhones and Android devices.
For this reason they are unsupported to access the broadcast. In addition, preferred internet browsers are Google Chrome or Mozilla Firefox. The workstation or laptop should be connected to a broadband internet connection and have a fully up-to-date version of Adobe Flash Player installed.
If you have any trouble logging on please email education@rsc.org.uk or call 01789 403432

Live Stream Only

Please note that the broadcast is only available to watch live. It will not be available to download and cannot be accessed at a later date.
If you are not able to watch the live broadcast with your students, contact your local cinema to find out if they are offering schools encore screenings.

Registration now open

Places for the broadcast are limited and to guarantee your school can take part you must register.
Please note that even if you have already signed up for email updates about the broadcast you still need to register your school to take part.

Registration Process

To complete the registration process you will be asked to provide contact details for a teacher at your school, the number of devices being used to access the broadcast, an estimated number of students who will be watching the broadcast and their Key Stage(s). Registration will close on 5th November, or as soon as all of the available places have been allocated.
